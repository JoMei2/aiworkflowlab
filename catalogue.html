Questions about the catalogue

Things people ask us about our programmes

Can programmes be delivered in-person or online?+
Both. The Copilot Workshop and Copilot Chat Workshop can be delivered in-person or online. The 12-session programmes are delivered as live interactive online sessions. We can also arrange hybrid delivery for larger teams across multiple locations.
Can you tailor programmes to our specific organisation?+
Yes. Every programme is adapted to your sector, your workflows, and your team's starting point. We run a short discovery call before any training to make sure examples and exercises are genuinely relevant to what your people do every day.
Are programmes suitable for people with no AI experience?+
Absolutely. Every programme is designed assuming no prior AI or technical knowledge. We introduce the simple RTO prompting framework first, then progressively unlock the full RTCCO framework as confidence grows. Nobody gets left behind.
What does the final project involve?+
For the 12-session programmes, each participant completes a final project chosen from a pre-agreed shortlist relevant to their role and sector. This ensures it is meaningful to the participant without being so bespoke it cannot be assessed consistently. Participants leave with a completed piece of work they can share with their manager as evidence of genuine capability.
Is there NHS or public sector funding available?+
Potentially, yes. ICB workforce budgets, PCN development funds, and Skills Bootcamp routes are all worth exploring depending on your organisation. Book a free call and we will help you think through the options for your setting.
What is the RTCCO prompting framework?+
RTCCO stands for Role, Task, Context, Constraints, Output. It is the prompting framework we use across all our programmes to help participants get consistently useful results from Copilot. We introduce it in stages - starting with the simpler RTO (Role, Task, Output) in the early sessions, then unlocking Context and Constraints as confidence grows. By the end of any 12-session programme every participant is using the full five-step framework confidently.
